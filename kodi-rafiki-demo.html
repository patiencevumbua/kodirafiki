Impact Areas Covered — and Where to See Each One in This Simulation

The six impact areas selected in the submission, each demonstrated live above.

✔Adopt to the leading work practices, trends and technology

See it: WhatsApp → menu E — every answer is retrieved from TRA's own documents and cites the section (📄 Chanzo). Type an off-topic question and watch the system refuse to invent an answer. Retrieval-based Swahili AI, hosted in Tanzania.

✔Compliance Costs

See it: all three phones — the trader complies from the phone they already own, in Swahili: no device to buy, no travel, no agent fees. Akiba ya Kodi turns TZS 600,000 into TZS 1,700 a day, and the SMS inbox keeps every record free of charge.

✔Expand tax base

See it: WhatsApp menu A, or the voice phone ("Sijajisajili bado") — TIN registration in one conversation, needing only NIDA, with first-year relief as the welcome. Receipt codes (menu D / SMS RISITI) make every buyer a reason for the next trader to register.

✔Improve service delivery

See it: the SMS phone (works on any handset, offline receipts at the lake shore) and the voice phone (a non-literate carpenter served entirely by speech, with an SMS record after every call). Populations no current TRA digital service can reach — served 24/7.

✔Minimize revenue collection costs

See it: the WhatsApp notification and the reminder that calls the carpenter — automated follow-up replacing manual block management. The mechanism was measured in Dar es Salaam: +18% payment at a 36:1 benefit-cost ratio.

✔Revenue Collection

See it: every payment moment above — a GePG control number issued inside the conversation, paid instantly by M-Pesa with the trader's own PIN, plus Tuzo ya Uzalendo lottery tickets making buyers demand receipts. Collection becomes the path of least resistance.
